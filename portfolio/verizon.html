class project: Fios Internet
This case study was my independent project submitted for a 'HCI for Tech Executives' class at Carnegie Mellon University. I used several UX research methods to improve the experience of purchasing internet from the Verizon Fios website.

Research Methods: stakeholder mapping, contextual inquiry, affinity clustering, journey mapping, storyboards, speed dating, prototyping, think-aloud usability testing.

Tools Used: Figma, Miro.

Key Problems: Distrust in ISP's, Uncertainty in Pricing Plans
In a series of contextual inquiries, participants walked through the process of purchasing internet from Fios and a competitor.

During the exercise, participants expressed a substantial level of distrust towards internet service providers and had concerns about hidden costs. Additionally, participants felt they were unable to adequately compare pricing plans.

Example Themes from Affinity Diagram: Distrust in ISPs & Desire for more information about pricing plans.

Crafting a Solution: Speed-Dating & Storyboards
I used the design sprint method "crazy 8's" to draft potential solutions to key issues identified during contextual inquiry. Using insights from the contextual inquiries, I drafted multiple possible solutions to address 1) improving trust in ISP's and 2) differentiating pricing plans. Testing: Prototyping and User Think-Alouds
Ultimately, I created two novel features to improve the user's experience: 1) a customer review page, and 2) a more thorough comparison of different pricing plans, modeled after a competitor's method (which participants liked in the contextual inquiries).

I tested the usability of an interactive prototype with multiple think-aloud interviews.

Future Considerations
If I were to continue this project, here's some ways I would have addressed user feedback:
  • Further highlight plan differences by reducing repetitive text in the comparison table.
  • Improve the trustworthiness/authenticity of user reviews by 1) adding a "verified customer" badge and 2) use actual (not hypothetical) customer feedback quotes.
  • Adopt a star-based review system to mirror common UX functions, as opposed to creating a novel review system where ratings were represented with the Verizon check.
