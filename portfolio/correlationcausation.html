correlation & causation discrimination
My dissertation, my swan song, the final boss battle of my PhD.

Across three studies, I tested the efficacy of an intervention to improve correlation-causation discrimination in undergraduate students. The short version of the first two studies: my intervention didn't work. So in the third study, I tried to figure out why.

Research Methods: Survey development.

Tools Used: Qualtrics.

Problem: Causal Theory Error
People, be they scientists or the general public, often make erroneous causal claims from correlational data. And science itself may be to blame — a fellow causality researcher once lamented that Research Methods instructors create "little monsters", aka students that repeat the phrase "correlation doesn't imply causation" (ad nauseum) without understanding why.

Causal theory error is a prolific issue in news headlines. In a world with endless access to rapidly generated information online (including misinformation), the ability to evaluate the validity of a causal claim is imperative for both researchers and the general public.
